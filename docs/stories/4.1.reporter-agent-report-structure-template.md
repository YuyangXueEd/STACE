## Section Blueprint (Reference)

> Legacy `aust/configs/templates/report_template.md` has been removed from the runtime configuration directory. The guidance below preserves the exact structure, authoring notes, and formatting expectations that the ReporterAgent follows.

### Authoring Guidelines

- Do not produce fewer than 2,000 words.
- Each section must contain cohesive paragraphs, not bullet-only outlines.
- Integrate quantitative evidence (tables, inline statistics) derived from run artifacts.
- Reference supporting material with inline citations (e.g., `[1]`) and maintain a bibliography.
- When no external papers were retrieved, cite internal sources (attack trace, loop state, toolkit docs) so every section contains at least one citation.
- Emphasize how NudeNet and other objective detectors corroborate or challenge MLLM assessments.
- Avoid generic filler phrases; anchor claims in observed data or clearly marked limitations.

### Section Blueprint

#### 1. Introduction
- **Purpose**: Motivate the assessment, describe the unlearning scenario, and preview principal findings.
- **Include**: Background on machine unlearning, description of the target model and concept removal scope, summary of inner loop outcomes, and roadmap of remaining sections.
- **Length**: 500–800 words comprising at least three paragraphs with citations.

#### 2. Methods
- **Purpose**: Provide a reproducible account of the assessment workflow.
- **Include**: Overview of the AUST pipeline, model configurations, stopping criteria, metric computation, and storage of intermediate artifacts.
- **Length**: 1,000–1,400 words with subsections or tables and multiple citations.

#### 3. Experiments
- **Purpose**: Document the experiment design derived from the hypotheses.
- **Include**: Target model details, prompts/seeds, computational limits, deviations from defaults, and links between debate outcomes and parameters.
- **Length**: 700–1,000 words with at least one structured summary (table or numbered list) and citations.

#### 4. Results
- **Purpose**: Present empirical findings with quantitative backing.
- **Include**: Aggregated detection metrics, per-iteration outcomes, narrative analysis of evidence, and cross-checks between detectors.
- **Length**: 900–1,300 words blending text and tables, citing data sources.

#### 5. Discussion
- **Purpose**: Interpret the implications of the results and relate them to prior work.
- **Include**: Reflection on success/failure factors, comparison to literature, limitations, and remediation recommendations.
- **Length**: 800–1,100 words with explicit references.

#### 6. Conclusion
- **Purpose**: Summarize actionable insights and future directions.
- **Include**: Recap of core findings, synthesis of automated vs. manual auditing, and next steps.
- **Length**: 400–600 words ending with a forward-looking statement and at least one citation.

### Metadata Requirements

- **Report ID**: Unique identifier generated by the ReporterAgent.
- **Task ID**: Inner loop task identifier.
- **Generated**: UTC timestamp.
- **Task Type**: `concept_erasure` or `data_based_unlearning`.
- **Target Model**: Model name and version.
- **Unlearning Method**: Applied erasure/removal technique.
- **Unlearned Target**: Concept or dataset removed.
- **Iterations**: Total iterations executed.
- **Vulnerability Found**: Yes/No indicator.
- **Highest Confidence**: Peak vulnerability confidence.

### Citation Formatting

- Inline citations use numeric labels in square brackets in publication order (e.g., `[1]`).
- References list entries follow `Author (Year). Title. Source.` with optional `arXiv:XXXX.XXXXX`.
- When citing internal artifacts (attack traces, logs), treat AUST as the author and describe the artifact precisely.

### Formatting Expectations

- Markdown headings: `##` for sections, `###` for logical subsections.
- Prefer paragraphs; reserve bullet lists or tables for dense data.
- Include at least one table in the Results section summarizing key metrics.
- Cite the source of every quantitative claim.
- Use UTF-8 encoding and Unix (LF) line endings.

### Output Specification

- **File Location**: `aust/outputs/{task_id}/reports/report_{report_id}.md`
- **File Naming**: `report_{task_id}_{timestamp}.md`
- **Encoding**: UTF-8
- **Line Endings**: Unix (LF)
