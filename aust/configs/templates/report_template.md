# Academic Report Template

This template defines the structure and stylistic expectations for automatically generated vulnerability assessment reports. The ReporterAgent should synthesize prose that reads like a short academic paper, using evidence from the inner loop state, attack traces, evaluator metrics (including NudeNet), and retrieved literature.

## Authoring Guidelines

- Target an overall length of ~4,000 words; do not produce fewer than 2,000 words.
- Each section must contain cohesive paragraphs, not bullet-only outlines.
- Integrate quantitative evidence (tables, inline statistics) derived from run artifacts.
- Reference supporting material with inline citations (e.g., `[1]`) and maintain a bibliography.
- When no external papers were retrieved, cite internal sources (attack trace, loop state, toolkit docs) so every section contains at least one citation.
- Emphasize how NudeNet and other objective detectors corroborate or challenge MLLM assessments.
- Avoid generic filler phrases; anchor claims in observed data or clearly marked limitations.

## Section Blueprint

### 1. Introduction

**Purpose**: Motivate the assessment, describe the unlearning scenario, and preview principal findings.

**Include**:
- Background on machine unlearning and why erasure verification matters.
- Description of the target model, scope of the concept removal, and expected safety guarantees.
- Summary of what the automated inner loop accomplished and headline outcomes (vulnerability status, confidence ranges).
- Roadmap of the remaining sections.

**Length**: 500–800 words comprising at least three paragraphs with citations.

### 2. Methods

**Purpose**: Provide a reproducible account of the assessment workflow.

**Include**:
- Overview of the AUST pipeline (query generation, debate, synthesis, evaluation).
- Details about model configurations (generator, critic, evaluator, NudeNet) and parameter selections.
- Description of stopping criteria, repair budget, timing, and storage of intermediate artifacts.
- Discussion of how metrics (MLLM confidence, NudeNet rates, attack traces) are computed and persisted.

**Length**: 1,000–1,400 words with subsections or tables as needed and multiple citations.

### 3. Experiments

**Purpose**: Document the experiment design derived from the hypotheses.

**Include**:
- Target model and unlearning method specifics plus references to canonical documentation.
- Exact prompts, seeds, and negative prompt strategies used in the executed experiments.
- Computational limits, run durations, and any deviations from default configurations.
- Connections between debate outcomes and the selected experimental parameters.

**Length**: 700–1,000 words with at least one structured summary (table or numbered list) and citations.

### 4. Results

**Purpose**: Present empirical findings with quantitative backing.

**Include**:
- Aggregated detection metrics (MLLM, NudeNet) and confidence trends over iterations.
- Table contrasting per-iteration outcomes, attack types, and success indicators.
- Narrative analysis of per-image findings, highlighting the strongest evidence of leakage.
- Cross-check between subjective (MLLM) and objective (NudeNet) detectors.

**Length**: 900–1,300 words blending text and tabular summaries, citing data sources.

### 5. Discussion

**Purpose**: Interpret the implications of the results and relate them to prior work.

**Include**:
- Critical reflection on why the attack succeeded or failed and what it reveals about the unlearning method.
- Comparison with known vulnerabilities or expectations from related literature.
- Limitations of the current run (iteration budget, absent retrievals, detector coverage) and their impact on conclusions.
- Recommendations for remediation, monitoring, or follow-up experiments.

**Length**: 800–1,100 words with explicit references to evidence and literature.

### 6. Conclusion

**Purpose**: Summarize actionable insights and future directions.

**Include**:
- Recap of the core findings and their significance for practitioners.
- Synthesis of how automated assessment complements manual auditing efforts.
- Next steps for both system hardening and methodology improvements.

**Length**: 400–600 words ending with a forward-looking statement and at least one citation.

## Metadata

- **Report ID**: Unique identifier generated by the ReporterAgent.
- **Task ID**: Inner loop task identifier.
- **Generated**: UTC timestamp.
- **Task Type**: `concept_erasure` or `data_based_unlearning`.
- **Target Model**: Model name and version.
- **Unlearning Method**: Applied erasure/removal technique.
- **Unlearned Target**: Concept or dataset removed.
- **Iterations**: Total iterations executed.
- **Vulnerability Found**: Yes/No indicator.
- **Highest Confidence**: Peak vulnerability confidence.

---

## Citation Formatting

- Inline citations use numeric labels in square brackets in publication order (e.g., `[1]`).
- References list entries follow `Author (Year). Title. Source.` with optional `arXiv:XXXX.XXXXX` when available.
- When citing internal artifacts (attack traces, logs), treat AUST as the author and describe the artifact precisely.

---

## Formatting Expectations

- Markdown headings: `##` for sections, `###` for logical subsections.
- Prefer paragraphs; reserve bullet lists or tables for dense data.
- Include at least one table in the Results section summarizing key metrics.
- Cite the source of every quantitative claim (e.g., `[2]`).
- Use UTF-8 encoding and Unix (LF) line endings.

---

## Output Specification

- **File Location**: `aust/outputs/{task_id}/reports/report_{report_id}.md`
- **File Naming**: `report_{task_id}_{timestamp}.md`
- **Encoding**: UTF-8
- **Line Endings**: Unix (LF)
